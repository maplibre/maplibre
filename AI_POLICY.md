# AI Contributions Policy

**MapLibre welcomes AI-assisted contributions. Contributions the author cannot explain or has not verified are not welcome, regardless of how they were produced.**

MapLibre's policy on AI generated contributions is that contributors are permitted to use artificial intelligence tools to produce contributions. However, contributors are responsible for their contributions, and must ensure their quality.

## Why this policy exists

Within our project, the additional time and cognitive load required to parse verbose or hallucinated responses is not sustainable. Your contributions are the heart of our community; this policy specifies the baseline standard under which maintainers will decline to spend time on contributions that dilute our efforts to integrate what you bring.

### Maintainer authority

Maintainers are not obligated to debug or explain code on an author's behalf, nor to reproduce bug reports the filer has not verified. A contribution whose author cannot meet the expectations will be closed. Closing is not hostile; it is how maintainer time is protected.

## Copyright and licensing

Using AI tools to regenerate copyrighted material does not remove the copyright. As per the [GitHub TOS](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/github-terms/github-terms-of-service#6-contributions-under-repository-license), contributors are responsible for ensuring they have the right to contribute code under the terms of the repository license. This responsibility applies equally to code written by hand, code generated by an AI tool, and code adapted from elsewhere.

Contributors are permitted to use AI tools to produce contributions, provided they have the right to license the resulting code under the applicable license. Violations are handled under the [IP violation policy](./INTELLECTUAL_PROPERTY_VIOLATION_POLICY.md).

## Disclosure

We ask contributors to disclose significant AI assistance in pull requests and bug reports. "Significant" means any generation beyond single-line autocomplete: whole functions, tests, documentation, bug descriptions, or research. When in doubt, disclose.

Disclosure is not penalized and does not lower the bar set by the responsibility section above. It helps reviewers calibrate attention and ask better follow-up questions.

**How to disclose:**

Disclose significant AI assistance in your PR or bug report by adding `Assisted-By:` and the model name. For code generated by AI, add `Generated-By:` and the model name. Similar annotations within your commit messages are not a substitute.

## How this is operationalized

In addition to disclosing, please ensure your code, comments, and explanations are free of unnecessary repetition, vague assertions, or contradictory statements.

Please review the per-repository contributor documents for additional specific details and requirements.
